Instructions

Quick Start

kataomoi is a webapp for finding people you're interested in who are interested in you, for any definition of “interested”. To use it, follow these steps, or check out the more detailed explanation below.

  1. Choose a room name (1–32 letters, numbers, periods, hyphens, and underscores, no spaces) and a username (same requirements), and sign in.
  2. Invite a group of friends to sign into the same room, by telling them the room name or sending them the invitation link. Note that room names (and usernames) are case-sensitive.
  3. Look at the list of usernames, and make sure you know which usernames correspond to which real people.
  4. Once everyone's signed in, if you're feeling security-conscious, make sure everyone has the same verification phrase.
  5. Check off the boxes for people you're interested in, and leave the remaining boxes blank.
  6. Click “Send Choices” and wait for all of the results to arrive.
  7. A Yes means you and another user are mutually interested in each other, a No means one of you isn't interested. Do what you want with this information, just don't be a jerk.
  8. Repeat with another (or the same) group of friends whenever you want!

Detailed Instructions

To get started with kataomoi, choose a room name and a username. A room name and a username can each be any string of 1–32 letters, numbers, periods, hyphens, and underscores (no spaces). Uppercase and lowercase letters are both allowed, and all names are case-sensitive. Enter your room name and username on the main page, and (assuming your name isn't already in use in the room you want) you'll be signed into the room you chose.

Now, you need to invite some friends to join you! Unlike dildo.io, the MIT-only matchmaking webapp that beat us to a public release by several months (not that we're bitter or anything), kataomoi only works with groups of users who are all online at the same time. Unfortunately, this is a fundamental limitation of the SENPAI cryptographic matchmaking protocol, which kataomoi is based on (for more details, see the About section). So, to use kataomoi, you'll need to get together a group of people that includes whoever you're interested in; this might be your living group, a living group you hang out with a lot, or just some sort of circle of friends. You all need to be signed in at the same time, but you don't necessarily all have to be in the same place (although, if you're especially security-conscious, being in the same physical room will be helpful; we'll explain more in a moment).

Your friends will need to be in the same virtual "room" as you. To invite them, you can tell them what room name you chose, or just send them the invitation link that appears when you sign in.

Once you and your friends are all signed into the same room, you'll see a list of their usernames with checkboxes next to them. Make absolutely sure you know which usernames correspond to which real people, or you might end up confessing your undying interest in the wrong person entirely. kataomoi doesn't enforce any constraints on usernames, except that they have to be 1–8 characters long. In particular, kataomoi usernames do not have to match users' MIT Kerberos usernames. We have no idea who you are or what your Kerberos is; we have no idea whether you're even an MIT student, and we really don't care either way. Being in the same physical place and talking to each other is a pretty foolproof way to figure out who's using what name, but if you're not all together, you can use some other communication channel to confirm names. Be warned: if your communications aren't securely authenticated somehow, it's always possible that someone is impersonating one of your friends and faking messages from them. Proceed at your own risk.

At the bottom of the page, below the list of usernames, you'll also see a verification phrase. The verification phrase is a 32-word nonsense phrase derived from the usernames in your virtual room and their associated cryptographic keys. If someone signed into the same room as you sees a different verification phrase, SOMETHING IS VERY WRONG AND YOU SHOULD PANIC. In particular, this means that one of the following is true:

  • We, the operators of kataomoi, have turned evil and are trying to spy on or falsify your selections.
  • Someone has taken over our server and is trying to spy on or falsify your selections.
  • Someone is using the SSL-vulnerability-of-the-month to intercept and modify web traffic between your browser and our server in order to spy on or falsify your selections.
  • There's a bug in our webapp.
We hope that none of these things ever happen, and have taken what precautions we can to prevent them. If you're not concerned about any of the first three possibilities, then feel free to ignore the verification phrase. However, if you're a security-conscious person like us, we recommend having your group take turns reading a few words of the phrase, and make sure you all agree (this is where being in the same physical place comes in handy). Note that the phrase will change whenever a new user joins the room.

Even if you can't check the verification phrase with everyone, checking it with as many people as you can is still helpful. If any subset of your group of people have the same verification phrase, then any communications among at least that subset are guaranteed secure (or at least, as close to guaranteed as anything is in this complicated and confusing world of buggy software and broken protocols).

Once you're satisfied that everything's secure, time to select who you're interested in! Go through the list of usernames, and if you're interested in someone, check off the box in the “DTF?” column; otherwise, leave it blank. (Note: “DTF” means nothing. “DTF” means everything. “DTF” means whatever you want it to mean. kataomoi is not exclusively, or even primarily, designed for finding sexual partners; our only reason for using “DTF” is to comply with tradition.)

When you're done selecting people, click the “Send Choices” button; the code running in your browser will perform cryptographic exchanges with the code running in everyone else's browsers, and eventually give you a “Yes” or “No” answer for each user in the list. A Yes answer indicates mutual interest; a No answer indicates that at least one of you isn't interested in the other one. Therefore, if you leave someone's checkbox blank, you're guaranteed to see a No answer for that person. If you check someone's checkbox, then the answer you see indicates whether they checked your box or not.

Once you've found out everyone's answers, what you do next is all up to you. If you found a match, we recommend going and talking to them, either immediately or shortly afterward, and figuring out what kind of things you'd like to do together. “Hey,” you might say, “So, um, it looks like we matched on kataomoi? Want to go grab dinner at Veggie Galaxy sometime and talk about things?” Other appropriate responses include (with proper consent) making out on the spot, falling into a sweet embrace filled with burning passion, or grabbing a duffel bag full of clothes and a box of matches and riding off into the night together on a stolen motorcycle.

And, if you find out that someone you're interested in isn't interested in you: feel free to deal with that in whatever way is healthiest for you, but just please, don't be a jerk. People feel the way they feel, and there isn't anything you can do about it, and sometimes it's terrible and sucky, but that's just the way it goes. You are a beautiful amazing person, and we believe in you, and we know that someday you'll find whatever it is you're looking for. You got this.

Of course, you can always use kataomoi again later with another (or the same) group of people. Your username and room name aren't permanent; your browser forgets all of it once you close the tab, and once everyone's gone, the room disappears.

Now, what are you waiting for? Grab your closest friends and most hated enemies and get started! Find love! Find lust! Find someone to go biking with! The world is at your cryptographically-secure fingertips!
